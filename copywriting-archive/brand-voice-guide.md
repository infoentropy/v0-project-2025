# Brand Voice Guide — Hearthstone Home Co.

**Document status:** Approved
**Owner:** Jordan Lee, Brand
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-10

---

## 1. Brand Personality

Hearthstone Home Co. makes considered, well-crafted home goods for people who
care about their living spaces. The brand sits at the intersection of quality
and warmth — never cold and corporate, never overly familiar or trend-chasing.

If the brand were a person, it would be a trusted friend who happens to have
excellent taste: knowledgeable without being a snob, direct without being pushy,
warm without being effusive.

---

## 2. Voice Dimensions

### 2.1 Warm, not gushing
We care about our customers and we show it — but we don't perform enthusiasm.
No exclamation point storms. No "We're SO excited to share this with you!!!"
Warmth comes through in word choice and rhythm, not punctuation.

| On-brand | Off-brand |
|---|---|
| "Good to see you again." | "We're SO glad you're back!!!" |
| "We think you'll find something worth coming back for." | "You're going to LOVE what we have in store!" |
| "We noticed you've been away." | "OMG, where have you been?!" |

### 2.2 Direct, not blunt
We get to the point. We don't bury the lead. But we don't strip out all
humanity in the name of brevity — a sentence of context earns the CTA.

| On-brand | Off-brand |
|---|---|
| "Your 15% off expires tonight at 11:59 pm PT." | "Discount code: SAVE15. Valid through 5/12." |
| "We'd like to make your return worth it." | "Buy now and save." |
| "There's something here worth seeing." | "Click here." |

### 2.3 Confident, not boastful
We stand behind what we make. We don't need to oversell it. Superlatives
weaken copy — if something is genuinely good, we describe it specifically
rather than labelling it "amazing" or "incredible."

| On-brand | Off-brand |
|---|---|
| "New collections have landed since your last visit." | "Our most amazing collection ever has arrived!" |
| "Exclusive to returning customers — not on the general list." | "The most incredible offer we've ever made!" |
| "Crafted to last." | "Unbelievable quality at unbeatable prices!" |

### 2.4 Respectful, not sycophantic
We don't flatter customers. Phrases like "You deserve the best" or
"As a valued customer" sound hollow and transactional. Instead, we treat
customers as intelligent adults who can evaluate things for themselves.

| On-brand | Off-brand |
|---|---|
| "As one of our past customers, you're getting this offer first." | "As a valued customer, you deserve only the best!" |
| "If our emails aren't right for you anymore, you're in control of that." | "We'd hate to lose you! Please don't go!" |
| "One order, 15% off, no code needed." | "We've worked incredibly hard to bring you this amazing deal!" |

## 3. Tone by Context

The core voice stays consistent. Tone adjusts to context.

| Context | Tone | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Re-engagement / win-back | Warm, unhurried | No guilt. No "you haven't opened our emails." Start with recognition, not the ask. |
| Promotional / offer | Confident, direct | Lead with the offer's value. State the expiry specifically (date + time + timezone). Never invent urgency that isn't real. |
| Expiry / last chance | Urgent, matter-of-fact | State the deadline once, clearly. Don't repeat it within the same email. No all-caps, no emotional manipulation. |
| Transactional (order confirm, shipping) | Efficient, reassuring | Short. No marketing language. Confirm the key details: what, when, how to get help. |
| Welcome / onboarding | Warm, orienting | Tell them what to expect. Avoid overwhelming with options. |
| Product launch | Curious, specific | Describe the product specifically rather than labelling it. Let the product earn the adjectives. |

## 4. Words and Phrases to Use

These have been reviewed and approved for use across campaigns.

**Relationship / recognition:**
- "We noticed"
- "It's been a little while"
- "Good to see you again"
- "Welcome back"
- "Still here"
- "We've been thinking of you"
- "We picked this for you"

**Offer framing:**
- "Exclusive" (only when it's genuinely exclusive — do not use for general list offers)
- "Your [offer]" (possessive framing — it belongs to the reader)
- "Applied at checkout" / "no code needed"
- "Expires [specific date + time + timezone]"

**Product / quality:**
- "Considered"
- "Crafted"
- "Designed to last"
- "Worth keeping"
- "Made for everyday use"

**CTAs:**
- Action verbs first: "See," "Claim," "Shop," "Use," "Explore"
- 2–4 words maximum
- Specific over generic: "Claim your 15% off" over "Shop now"

## 5. Words and Phrases to Avoid

### Weakening language
These words reduce conviction without adding softness.

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "just" | Minimises the thing it describes. "We just wanted to say hi" feels apologetic. |
| "really" | Vague intensifier. Use a better adjective or cut it. |
| "very" | Same as above. |
| "a little bit" | Hedging. Be direct. |
| "hopefully" | Signals uncertainty. We're confident in what we offer. |

### Hollow superlatives
| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "amazing" | Overused to the point of meaninglessness. Describe specifically instead. |
| "incredible" | Same as above. |
| "unbelievable" | Same as above — also slightly adversarial. |
| "the best" (as a standalone claim) | Requires proof. If you mean it, show it. |
| "world-class" | Meaningless without evidence. |

### Manipulative or alarmist language
| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "FINAL WARNING" | Aggressive. Not our tone. |
| "You haven't opened our emails" | Accusatory. Makes the reader feel surveilled, not cared for. |
| "Don't miss out" | Overused. Reframe as what they gain, not what they'd lose. |
| "Act now" | Pushy. Use the specific deadline instead. |
| "Limited time only" (without specifics) | Vague urgency feels manufactured. Always give the actual expiry. |

### Sycophantic phrases
| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "As a valued customer" | Formulaic. Everyone is a "valued customer." |
| "You deserve the best" | Hollow flattery. |
| "We'd hate to lose you" | Guilt-tripping. |
| "We're so grateful for your support" | Transactional — save for genuine thank-you moments, not offers. |

### Informal / off-brand register
| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "Hey!" as an opener | Too casual for the brand register. Use the first name or skip the opener. |
| "Wanna," "gonna," "kinda" | Not our register. |
| "Obsessed with" | Trend-speak. Fades quickly. |
| "Dropping" (for product launches) | Streetwear-adjacent. Not our category. |

## 6. Grammar and Style Conventions

- **Oxford comma:** Always. "Chairs, tables, and shelves" — not "Chairs, tables and shelves."
- **Em dash:** Use for emphasis or a beat pause (—), not a hyphen (-). Space on both sides.
- **Contractions:** Allowed and encouraged in conversational contexts. "We've," "it's," "you're."
- **Sentence fragments:** Allowed sparingly for rhythm. "No pressure, no pitch." works. Don't overuse.
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine. Use numerals for 10 and above and for all percentages ("15% off").
- **Time format:** 12-hour clock with am/pm lowercase and timezone: "11:59 pm PT."
- **Date format:** Day + month + year, no ordinals: "12 May 2026" not "May 12th, 2026."
- **Ellipsis:** Avoid. If the thought is unfinished, finish it or cut it.
- **Exclamation marks:** Maximum one per email, and only if the moment genuinely warrants it. Never in subject lines.

---

## 7. Subject Line Rules

- **Length:** ≤ 50 characters (including personalisation tokens at their rendered length).
- **Capitalisation:** Sentence case only. Never title case ("We Picked This For You") or all-caps.
- **Personalisation:** First name token (`{{first_name}}`) is approved. Do not use purchase history or behavioural tokens in subject lines without Legal sign-off.
- **Emojis:** Brand discretion — maximum one per subject line. Must add meaning, not decoration. Never in preview text.
- **Punctuation:** No exclamation marks. A question mark is acceptable if the question is genuine. Avoid ellipsis.
- **What works:** Specificity, recognition, clear value. What doesn't: vague teasers ("You won't believe this…"), fake personalisation, manufactured urgency.

---

## 8. CTA Button Copy Rules

- **Length:** 2–4 words.
- **Format:** Action verb first, sentence case.
- **Specificity:** The button should tell the reader where they're going or what they're getting.

| Approved patterns | Avoid |
|---|---|
| "See what's new" | "Click here" |
| "Claim your 15% off" | "Shop now" (acceptable only when no better option exists) |
| "Use your discount" | "Learn more" (vague) |
| "Explore the collection" | "Buy" (too transactional, no context) |

## 9. Legal and Compliance Copy

The following copy is mandatory in all promotional emails. Do not alter wording
without Legal sign-off.

**Unsubscribe footer (all emails):**
> You're receiving this email because you opted in at hearthstonehome.com.
> [Unsubscribe](#) · [View in browser](#) · Hearthstone Home Co., 240 Market Street, Suite 400, San Francisco, CA 94105.

**Promotional offer disclaimer (emails containing discounts or offers):**
> [Offer details: discount amount, order minimum if any]. Single use. Expires
> [date]. Cannot be combined with other offers. Exclusions apply.
> [Full terms](#).

Both blocks are required verbatim. The offer disclaimer must match the offer
exactly — do not reuse a disclaimer from a previous campaign without updating
the terms.
